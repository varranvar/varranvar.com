return

a minimal tabletop role-playing game suited for online play

  • Motivation:
    • Traditional TTRPGs, while possessing a certain charm in person, have too many complex character sheets and tedious rules to hold a player's attention in online play
    • Simpler TTRPGs, like Honey Heist have less friction and are more story-telling oriented, which can create a more immersive experience for the players
  • Design:
    • Character are defined by a list of properties, such as proficiencies, deficiencies, traits, physical attributes, relationships, items, and any other relevant information
      • For example, Turtle-Person:
        • is a turtle-human hybrid
          • has a hard shell
          • is amphibious
          • is vegan
          • is slow on land
          • is fast in water
        • is stealthy
        • has a wooden staff
    • When an player wants to make an action:
      1. Have them roll a 6-sided dice
      2. If the character has a relevant property that would give them an advantage or disadvantage in the situation, have them roll a second 6-sided dice and pick the highest or lowest roll accordingly
        • For example, Turtle-Person would make a disadvantage roll when running away from an enemy but an advantage roll when swimming away
      3. 1 is a great failure, 2 is a failure, 3 is a slight failure, 4 is a slight success, 5 is a success, and 6 is a great success
