On Life-Logging

I spent two years tracking everything I did, every minute of the day.

I did this by writing entries in a text file. Each entry had a starting timestamp, an ending timestamp, and a natural language description. Here's a sample: 


  02/12/22 01:14 02/12/22 10:00 I slept.
  02/12/22 10:00 02/12/22 10:24 I ate breakfast.
  02/12/22 10:24 02/12/22 11:51 I wrote in my wiki about Project G.
  02/12/22 11:51 02/12/22 12:09 I made coffee and talked to my mother.

I also wrote a program that would graph the number of a times a keyword appeared in my log over time, which allowed me to visualize my behavior.

I eventually came to the reasonable conclusion that life-logging is not useful. Tracking sleep, diet, and fitness can help optimize health. Tracking productivity, mood, and social activity may also lend some insights. Other than that, there's nothing to be learned from life-logging. It's interesting to have a record of what you were doing at any point in time, but not interesting enough to be worth the effort.
